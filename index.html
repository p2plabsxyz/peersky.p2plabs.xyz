Have you ever wondered what it would be like if the internet wasn't controlled by a handful of big companies? That's what the distributed web, or DWeb, is all about. It‚Äôs a return to the internet‚Äôs original spirit, operating on a network of computers where everyone shares and collaborates. This way, no single entity can control or shut it down. It's like turning the entire web into a giant global community project.

Peersky is your experimental personal gatekeeper to a new way of accessing web content. Imagine a world where you can access web pages, communicate securely, and store or share files directly from a global network of peers, without relying on centralized servers. With Peersky, this vision becomes a reality.

It works by leveraging multiple protocols like IPFS, Hypercore, and Web3. Peersky offers a variety of local P2P applications optimized for specific tasks. For instance, peersky://p2p/chat facilitates secure, peer-to-peer messaging, while peersky://p2p/upload is used for decentralized file storage. With peersky://p2p/editor, anyone can effortlessly build and publish websites. For nerds, peersky://p2p/wiki provides a search interface for browsing Wikipedia over IPFS. Additionally, the social reader, a peer-to-peer, offline ActivityPub client, allows you to read and follow microblogs on the fediverse, functioning like a social media RSS feed for the distributed web. As Peersky continues to grow, new protocols such as Veilid and BitTorrent, along with additional P2P apps, will be added, further expanding its capabilities and appeal.

Peersky is designed for everyone, making it easy for users owners to explore the web while enhancing privacy and digital autonomy‚Äîwithout relying on traditional internet infrastructure.
